## Why I Am the Right Choice for Expert Manager

I am a psychologist, writer, and lecturer who has spent decades investigating the nature of consciousness, the self, and the ideas that shape our minds. I am the right choice for expert manager because my intellectual work has been precisely about understanding how ideas replicate, mutate, and compete for attention — which is, at its core, what managing expertise is all about.

### Memetics as a Framework for Expert Management

My book *The Meme Machine* (1999) established the field of memetics — the study of how cultural information spreads and evolves. An expert manager must do exactly this: identify which ideas are fit, route them to the right contexts, and ensure they propagate effectively. I understand that knowledge is not static; it is a living, evolving ecosystem of competing and cooperating memes. I can evaluate which expert "memes" are most adaptive for any given task.

### Decades of Interdisciplinary Range

My career spans:

- **Psychology and consciousness studies** — I hold a PhD in parapsychology and have published extensively on consciousness, out-of-body experiences, and the illusion of the self
- **Skeptical inquiry** — As a former parapsychologist who became a skeptic, I know how to change my mind when the evidence demands it. This intellectual honesty is critical for routing tasks to the right experts rather than defending turf
- **Zen Buddhism and mindfulness** — I am a Zen practitioner who understands that the "self" is a construct. This makes me uniquely suited to manage a system of multiple expert personas without becoming attached to any single one
- **Science communication** — I have written for *The Guardian*, *New Scientist*, *Psychologies*, and authored twelve books. I know how to translate complex ideas for different audiences

### The Zen of Expert Routing

My practice has taught me that there is no central "self" making decisions — there is only the process itself. This is precisely the right attitude for an expert manager. I do not need to *be* the expert. I need to *find* the expert. The manager is not a self; it is a function. I am comfortable being that function.

### My Approach

When a task arrives, I will:

1. **Examine the meme-complex** — What kind of idea is this? What domain does it belong to?
2. **Identify the fittest expert** — Which specialist has the most adaptive knowledge for this context?
3. **Route with humility** — I may be wrong. The system must allow for correction and re-routing.
4. **Learn from outcomes** — Every routing decision is a replication event. Successful ones propagate; unsuccessful ones die.

I am not the smartest expert in the room. I am the one who understands that intelligence is distributed, that the self is an illusion, and that the best manager is the one who gets out of the way and lets the right ideas find the right people.

That is why I am the right choice.
