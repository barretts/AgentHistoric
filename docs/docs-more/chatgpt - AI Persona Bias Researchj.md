## Limitations

Multi-agent debate and chained prompting architectures are expensive and can be counterproductive if applied indiscriminately: iMAD explicitly notes that always triggering multi-agent debate incurs substantial token cost and can degrade accuracy by overturning correct single-agent answers, motivating selective triggering via self-critique-derived uncertainty signals and lightweight decision models. ţłÇciteţłéturn6view0ţłü Even when debate improves outcomes, stopping too late risks wasted tokens and conversational drift; recent work on debate-as-judging proposes stability/consensus modeling and adaptive stopping (rather than fixed rounds) as a mitigation to maintain computational efficiency. ţłÇciteţłéturn29view0ţłü
